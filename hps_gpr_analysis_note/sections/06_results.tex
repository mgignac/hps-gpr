\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

The completed figures bundled with this note document the current state of the 2015,
2016 10\%, and 2015+2016 validation program, but they were generated before the final
adoption of the leakage-retaining signal-template convention described in
Section~\ref{sec:methodology}. They are therefore retained as organized, publication-style
placeholders for the main result classes rather than as the final frozen physics
outputs. The qualitative structure of the results is already clear, and the note is
written so that the figures can be replaced one-for-one when the rerun is complete.

\subsection{2015 analysis}
\label{sec:results-2015}

The completed 2015 study uses the engineering-run invariant-mass histogram over the
\SIrange{20}{130}{MeV} scan region, the 2015 mass-resolution parameterization, and the
2015 radiative-fraction treatment summarized in Section~\ref{sec:datasets}. The main
deliverables are the 95\% CL upper-limit bands in signal-yield and $\eps^2$ space,
supported by local/global significance bookkeeping and representative extraction
displays. Even before the final leakage-retaining rerun, the 2015 study already shows
that the global-GP workflow produces smooth scan products and a statistically coherent
mapping between the local extracted yield and the coupling limit.

development, toy generation, and extraction displays. Its detector-specific inputs

radiative-fraction parameterization, and the broader parent mass coverage of the 2016
run. Within that subset, the same statistical machinery is used: global GP prediction,
profiled local extraction, and \CLs\ limits in both yield and coupling space.

\subsection{2015+2016 10\% combined upper limit on \texorpdfstring{$\eps^2$}{epsilon2}}
\label{sec:results-combined-1516}

The first completed shared-$\eps^2$ combination joins the 2015 engineering run and the
2016 10\% validation subset through the joint likelihood described in
Section~\ref{sec:combination}. This is the first point in the note where the value of a
likelihood-level combination becomes explicit. The result is not constructed by
averaging single-dataset limits; it is obtained by fitting a common coupling directly
to the concatenated blinded-bin information. In the overlap region the combination
therefore gains sensitivity by adding statistically independent information while
preserving the correct dataset-specific mapping from $\eps^2$ to total signal yield.
